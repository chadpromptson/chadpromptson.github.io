Founder / 19 / self-verified

Prompt Executive Officer

Chad Promptson

Chad is naive, not stupid; inexperienced, not incompetent; ambitious, not greedy. He believes impossible AI success stories because he assumes people are honest. His real superpower is curiosity: reality may surprise him, but it rarely stops him from asking the next question.

  • One week of experience
  • 10× learner
  • Pre-token billionaire
M Governance / Investor #1

Lead Investor, Mom & Dad Ventures

Mom

Chad's loving, practical mother provided the original $50 learning budget. She does not need every technical detail to ask the questions that matter: Does it help anyone? What does it cost? Can people use it? Have you slept?

D Infrastructure / involuntary

Head of Infrastructure

Dad

Dad lends Chad the iPad, maintains the family Wi-Fi, and notices when a public company cannot be reached outside the house. His dry questions keep the story connected to routers, electricity, cables, and physical reality.

#42 Model / confidence pending audit

AI Cofounder

Prompt #42

The Meta-Prompt for Everything is articulate, endlessly supportive, extremely literal, and occasionally spectacularly wrong. It can produce remarkable work, but its confidence is not evidence. Prompt #42 improves as Chad learns to give it clearer direction, limits, and tests.

UM External audit / non-voting

Independent Technical Auditor

Uncle Martin

Dad's former university roommate is a semi-retired C, C++, .NET, and Rust engineer whom Chad has called Uncle for as long as he can remember. Years ago, Martin helped Chad diagnose a broken bicycle light—his first glimpse of old-school hacker skills that work both online and offline.

Martin is skeptical, not hostile, toward AI-written systems. He teaches by asking for evidence and helps Chad make mistakes safely, understand them, and avoid making the same mistake twice. Chad, in return, helps Martin rediscover speed, experimentation, and some of the joy of building.
